Commitment enforcement at the system level

In active development

Why it exists

Flexibility can make it easy to drift away from intentions. Most tools let you change the rules whenever you want. Oath is designed differently: when you've decided something matters, the system keeps you honest.

How it works

Oath operates at the operating-system level. Rules are enforced with technical rigor. Changing them requires deliberate, difficult steps, not just a click. The system respects your choices by making them hard to undo.

What Oath does

System-level support

Rules are enforced at the OS level, not easily bypassed or negotiated.

Accountability records

Complete transparency. You see what you've kept, and what you've let slide.

Deliberate friction

Changing your rules takes real work. That work is intentional. That's the point.

What Oath is not

Not a productivity tracker

No habit tracking, no streaks, no motivational rewards. Oath enforces commitments, it doesn't celebrate them.

Not optimized for comfort

Built for effect, not ease. Follow-through requires friction. The friction is the feature.

Status

Oath is in development. We're building the enforcement layer and running it through real-world testing. We're not rushing release. We're rushing nothing.

There are no public timelines. We release when the system is dependable.
